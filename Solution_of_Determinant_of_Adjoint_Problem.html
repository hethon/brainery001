$$ \textcolor{#9255F7}{\bold{Determinant \space of \space Adjoints}} $$ $ \textcolor{#C672A5}{\bold{Note:}} \\ $ Since the $|A| \not = 0$, $A^{-1}$ exists. By the definition of inverse of matrices, we have $$ \tag{*} A^{-1} = \frac{1}{|A|}adj A $$ Where $adj A$ is the $adjoint$ of $A. \\$ $\text{}\\$ $ \textcolor{#F98E56}{\bold{Solution: }} \\ $ Taking the determinant of $(*)$, we get $$ \begin{aligned} |A^{-1}| &= \Big|\frac{1}{|A|}adj A \Big| \\ &= \Big(\frac{1}{|A|}\Big)^3 |adj A| \\ \end{aligned} $$ $$ \begin{aligned} \tag{**} &= \frac{|adj A|}{|A|^{3}} \end{aligned} $$ Rearranging (**), we have $$ |adj A| = |A|^{3}|A^{-1}| $$ But we know that $$ |A^{-1}| = \frac{1}{|A|} $$ Therefore, $$ \begin{aligned} |adj A| &= |A|^{2} \\ &= 2^2 \\ &= \bold {\underline {4}} \end{aligned} $$
